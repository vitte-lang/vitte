# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We want Vitte to be a welcoming, professional, and respectful community for everyone.

All contributors, maintainers, and participants are expected to help create an environment where people can collaborate safely and productively.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive in discussions.
- Focus on technical issues, not personal attacks.
- Give feedback that is specific and actionable.
- Assume good intent, ask for clarification before escalating.
- Accept that people have different experience levels and communication styles.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, intimidation, or stalking.
- Insults, slurs, discriminatory language, or hate speech.
- Personal attacks, hostile trolling, or repeated bad-faith behavior.
- Publishing private information without consent (doxxing).
- Sexualized language or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Deliberate disruption of community discussions or project work.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues and pull requests
- discussions and review comments
- project chat/community channels
- any public or private communication representing the project

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

- report it to maintainers through repository contact channels
- include links, screenshots, and context when possible
- avoid public escalation when safety/privacy is at risk

Security-related reports should follow `SECURITY.md`.
General support requests should use `SUPPORT.md`.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for interpreting and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

Possible enforcement actions include:

- warning
- comment or content removal
- temporary interaction restrictions
- permanent ban from project spaces

Repeated or severe violations may lead to immediate removal.

## Enforcement Principles

When handling reports, maintainers aim to be:

- fair
- consistent
- proportionate to impact
- focused on community safety

## Attribution

This policy is inspired by common open-source community standards, including Contributor Covenant principles.
